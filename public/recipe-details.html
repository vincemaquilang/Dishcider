Method of cooking:

Combine high-quality flour with a precise amount of warm water, a pinch of salt, sugar, and yeast in a large mixing bowl. Stir the ingredients until they form a rough dough, ensuring all dry components are fully incorporated. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and knead it thoroughly, using both hands to press, fold, and stretch the mixture until it becomes smooth and elastic. This process should take about 8–10 minutes and ensures the dough develops the necessary structure for rising.

Once kneaded, shape the dough into a ball and place it into a lightly oiled bowl, turning it once to coat the surface and prevent it from drying out.

Cover the bowl with a clean, damp kitchen towel or plastic wrap and set it in a warm, draft-free location to rise. Allow the dough to double in size, which may take approximately one to two hours, depending on the ambient temperature. Once the dough has risen, punch it down gently to release trapped air, then transfer it back to the floured surface. Divide it into portions if making multiple loaves or rolls, and shape the dough into your preferred style, ensuring even thickness for uniform baking.

Arrange the shaped dough onto a baking tray or in a loaf pan lined with parchment paper or lightly greased.

Preheat your oven to the desired temperature—typically 375°F (190°C)—to ensure consistent heat for baking. Place the tray or pan in the oven, and bake the bread until it turns golden brown, with a crisp crust that sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom. This step usually takes 25–30 minutes, but times may vary based on size and oven efficiency. Once baked, remove the bread from the oven and transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely.

Cooling is essential to set the crumb structure and ensure the bread slices cleanly. Serve and enjoy with your favorite accompaniments!
